Who We Are

About PAYCON 2026

Africa's premier youth conference, uniting the continent's brightest minds to shape the next chapter of our shared story.

A Continent on the Rise

Africa is home to the world's youngest population - over 60% of Africans are under 25. This demographic dividend is not a challenge to manage; it is a transformative force waiting to be harnessed.

The Pan-African Youth Conference (PAYCON) 2026 is a flagship continental platform that brings together young leaders, innovators, policymakers, researchers, and entrepreneurs from all 54 African nations and the diaspora. Hosted at the symbolic heart of African unity - the African Union Headquarters in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia - PAYCON creates a powerful bridge between youth energy and institutional decision-making.

Aligned with the African Union's Agenda 2063 - Africa's blueprint for inclusive growth and sustainable development.

Contributing to the UN Sustainable Development Goals, especially SDG 8, 9, 10, 16 and 17.

Generating tangible policy declarations and action frameworks delivered directly to AU institutions.

Youth Power

Mobilizing Africa's 1.3 billion youth as the primary engine of continental transformation.

Innovation First

Showcasing homegrown solutions to Africa's most pressing economic and social challenges.

Policy Impact

Translating youth voices into actionable policy recommendations at the continental level.

Pan-African Unity

Building lasting networks across all 54 nations and the African diaspora worldwide.

3-Day Summit

Conference Program

Three transformative days of plenaries, panels, workshops, and networking - designed to inspire, connect, and catalyze action.

Day One - Defining Africa's Future

2 September 2026

08:00 Arrival, Accreditation & Welcome Coffee
Delegate registration, badge collection, and networking breakfast. Exhibition booths open.
09:30 Official Opening Ceremony
Welcome addresses from the AU Commissioner for Political Affairs, Heads of State video messages, PAYCON Convener's address, and cultural performances celebrating African heritage.
11:00 Keynote: Africa's Demographic Dividend
High-level keynote address on harnessing Africa's youth population as the continent's greatest strategic asset for the 21st century.
12:30 Networking Lunch & Cultural Showcase
Pan-African lunch with regional cuisine, cultural exhibitions, and structured networking.
14:00 Plenary: Agenda 2063 - Youth as Architects
Panel discussion with AU officials, youth leaders from across Africa, and international development partners on youth's role in achieving Agenda 2063 aspirations.
15:30 Breakout Sessions - Governance & Policy Tracks
Parallel sessions on Democratic Participation, Anti-Corruption Frameworks, and Youth in Government. Small group discussions with facilitators.
17:30 Evening Gala & Welcome Reception
Formal welcome gala with pan-African entertainment, networking dinner, and Award of Recognition ceremony for distinguished young African leaders.

Day Two - Innovation, Economy & Future of Work

3 September 2026

08:30 Morning Plenary: The Digital Africa Imperative
High-impact keynote on AI, fintech, and digital infrastructure as accelerators of African development. Live demo of African-built tech solutions.
10:00 Innovation Showcase & Startup Pitch Competition
African startups present disruptive solutions to a panel of investors and industry leaders. Live audience voting. Prize pool for top 3 startups.
12:00 AfCFTA: Youth & Intra-African Trade Forum
Deep-dive forum on how young Africans can leverage the African Continental Free Trade Area to build cross-border businesses and value chains.
13:30 Lunch & Career Fair
Career opportunities from leading African and international organizations. CV clinics, mentorship speed-meetings, and internship announcements.
14:30 Professional Skills Masterclasses
Parallel workshops: Public Speaking, Grant Writing, Data Science for Development, Social Entrepreneurship, and Leadership Frameworks.
16:30 Scientific Research Congress
Peer-reviewed research presentations from African scholars and students. Poster sessions, abstract presentations, and best paper awards.

Day Three - Sustainability, Partnerships & Continental Action

4 September 2026

08:30 Climate Action Forum: Africa's Green Future
High-level discussion on climate financing, green energy transition, and youth-led climate activism across the continent. COP Youth Caucus updates.
10:00 Health Innovation & Universal Healthcare Summit
Conversations on digital health, medical education in Africa, pandemic preparedness, and mental health for African youth. Hosted with FAMSA and APHSNI.
11:30 Partnership Forum: Building the Africa We Want
Strategic MOU signings, partnership announcements between youth organizations, governments, and international development agencies.
13:00 Mentorship Roundtables & Networking Lunch
Curated mentorship sessions connecting young delegates with senior African leaders, entrepreneurs, and international experts.
15:00 PAYCON 2026 Declaration Adoption
Adoption of the Addis Ababa Youth Declaration - a comprehensive action framework for youth-led development, to be formally submitted to the African Union Commission.
16:30 Closing Ceremony & Awards Gala
Closing addresses, awards for Best Research Paper, Outstanding Youth Leader, Innovation Champion, and Cultural Ambassador. Group photo and official closing.

Health & Safety

Yellow Fever vaccination certificate required for entry (check country-specific requirements).

Consult your healthcare provider 4–6 weeks before travel for recommended vaccinations.

24/7 emergency support line available to all registered delegates.

Travel Tips

Climate: Addis Ababa is mild year-round (15–25°C). September can be rainy - pack a light jacket.

Currency: Ethiopian Birr (ETB). USD, EUR widely accepted. ATMs available at airport and city.

Connectivity: SIM cards available at airport. Conference venue has dedicated high-speed Wi-Fi.

Language: Amharic is official. English widely spoken in business, hospitality, and academia.

Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can attend PAYCON 2026?

PAYCON 2026 is open to young Africans and friends of Africa aged 18–40 from all 54 African nations and the African diaspora worldwide. This includes students, young professionals, researchers, entrepreneurs, policymakers, civil society actors, media professionals, and more.

Do I need a visa to travel to Ethiopia?

Ethiopia offers Visa on Arrival for most African passport holders at approximately $60 USD. You can also apply for an e-Visa before departure. PAYCON 2026 can provide a support/invitation letter upon registration to assist with your visa application. The nearest airport is Addis Ababa Bole International Airport (ADD). Contact our team for details specific to your country.

How do I register for PAYCON 2026?

Registration opens 20 June 2026 and closes 20 August 2026. Click the "Register Now" button on this page or visit our registration portal. Complete the online form, submit required documents, and await your confirmation email. Early Bird registration closes 20 July 2026.

Are scholarships and grants available?

Yes! We have 100 sponsored scholarship slots available for deserving delegates. Scholarships may cover registration fees, accommodation, and in some cases, partial travel support. Apply through the scholarship application form during registration. Priority is given to delegates from LDCs and those demonstrating leadership potential.

Where exactly is the conference venue?

PAYCON 2026 will be held at the African Union Headquarters, Roosevelt Street, Addis Ababa, Ethiopia. The venue is approximately 15 km from Bole International Airport (ADD). Shuttle transfers will be arranged for registered delegates.

Can I submit a research paper or abstract?

Yes! PAYCON 2026 includes a Scientific Research Congress with abstract submissions open to all delegates. Selected abstracts will be presented as oral presentations or posters. All submissions undergo peer review by our Scientific Committee. Guidelines will be published with registration materials.

Will there be a virtual/hybrid attendance option?

We are working on a hybrid attendance option for delegates who cannot travel. Key plenary sessions and high-level panels will be live-streamed. Virtual delegates will have access to recordings, live Q&A, and networking features. Details will be announced closer to the conference.

How can my organization become a sponsor or partner?

We welcome organizational partnerships and sponsorships at all levels - from Platinum to Community Supporter. Visit our Sponsors section to see tier benefits, or contact us directly at the email in the Contact section to request our full sponsorship prospectus.
